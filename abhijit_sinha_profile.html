About

Research & Background

I am a Ph.D. researcher at the Department of Condensed Matter Physics (DCMP), Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Mumbai, working under the supervision of Prof. Shankar Ghosh. My thesis investigates non-equilibrium systems under geometric and informational constraints.

My work spans granular physics, active matter, and stochastic processes — using hands-on tabletop experiments and computational tools to uncover universal principles. From understanding how granular arches form and collapse in silos, to building robot swarms that exhibit emergent collective behavior, I aim to bridge physics and engineering.

Prior to my Ph.D., I completed an M.Sc. in Physics at TIFR Hyderabad, and a B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from Jadavpur University, Kolkata — a background that uniquely positions me at the interface of theoretical physics and experimental design.

Research Projects

Selected Work

🤖
40-Agent Swarm Robot Platform
Built a 40-agent swarm robot platform from scratch to study emergent collective motion. This custom-built platform enables investigation of how simple local interactions give rise to complex group behaviors — analogous to bird flocks and fish schools.
Robotics Active Matter Embedded C PCB Design
⚗️
Granular Clog Mitigation Experiments
Designed tabletop experiments demonstrating simple, intuitive clog mitigation strategies in granular flow — including an obstruction paradoxically facilitating flow, and a flexible string suppressing clogs. Led to a PRL publication and a patent filing.
Granular Physics Experiment Design High-speed Imaging
🎈
Weather Balloon Instrumentation System
Developing a multi-sensor instrumentation system for aerosol and atmospheric property measurements on a weather balloon platform — combining embedded systems, sensor fusion, and data acquisition for real-world environmental monitoring.
Instrumentation Raspberry Pi Atmospheric Physics
📐
Optimal Navigation in Noisy Environments
Investigated strategies for optimal navigation when environmental information is incomplete or noisy — connecting stochastic processes, information theory, and active matter physics. Currently under review (arXiv:2512.20336).
